Theory: The Systemic Tau Paradigm
=================================

The **Systemic Tau** framework proposes a fundamental shift in how we understand and measure time within complex systems. Instead of treating time as a homogeneous, external background (Chronos), it introduces the **Discrete Extramental Clock (RECD)**, an internal metric of time generated by the system's own structural reorganization (Kairos).

Three-Layer Ontology
--------------------

The framework operationalizes this concept through a strict mathematical and ontological three-layer architecture:

1. **Layer 1 (Local Intensification):**
   Measures local persistence through hyper-persistence of ordinal states and structural trapping using Recurrence Quantification Analysis (RQA).

2. **Layer 2 (Relational Coherence):**
   Identifies *Joint Episodes* (relational kairoi). This layer detects enabling windows where modules within the system become anti-synchronized, creating the necessary conditions for a global shift.

3. **Layer 3 (Ontological Ascent):**
   Detects the exact moment of global structural reorganization. By analyzing the distance between probability distributions (e.g., Kolmogorov-Smirnov contrasts) and network topology (Frobenius norm shifts), this layer flags the emergence of a new law of temporal discreteness.

Fractal Dimension
-----------------

The paradigm establishes that the accumulated discrete time $T$ converges to a fractal dimension $D \approx 1.98$, reflecting the geometric properties of the underlying chaotic dynamics defined by the Feigenbaum constant.
